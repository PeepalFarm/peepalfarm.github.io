Feed pigeons, dogs and cows in public places!

  • Robin Singh, Hindustan Times, New Delhi
    • | 
  • Updated: Jun 13, 2016 07:48 IST
Pigeon feeding is a very common practice on the roads of Delhi. (Sonu Mehta/ HT file photo)

Of the many acts that make Delhi a dream place for animal lovers, the practice of pigeon feeding is the most easy for you to follow.

Many public space are dedicated for people step to feed pigeons. Some bring their own grain. Others buy it from the pavement. Astrologers prescribe feeding millet and green lentils to pigeons as a way to attain happiness. 

Feeding birds and animals is a noble deed. It can be done at home and it can be done at street. Feed the street mutt along with your pet dog. Better still, adopt a street dog home, feed and look after it instead of buying a breed dog. 

Birds are natural foragers — picking food from trees, shrubs, grass, and catching worms and insects. With the loss of green and manure patches, the birds are deprived of natural food. So it is good to leave some, for your neighborhood birds in the balcony, and make a big show of it so others can follow your lead!

Living off discarded food stray dogs are natural scavengers who keep our city clean and free of rats.  

If we are really a city of do-gooders, we can do a lot better than just feeding stray dogs. We should get the dogs in our neighborhood sterilized. After all, a vaccinated and sterilized dog is an asset to the community.

When you make rotis at home, make the first one for a stray cow. After all they are on the street because of our dairy consumption, so the least we can do for them is to feed them rotis.

In this summer heat, lets not forget that animals not only need food but water too. Lets keep bowls of water easily accessible for large and small animals.

Let’s turn Delhi into an animal-friendly city by reducing outdoor lighting during the night, promoting green roofs, planting native plants, and not converting every natural space into manicured gardens. Put a sprinkling of grains by a water trough in your balcony and count the cheerful sparrows. You won’t worry about happiness.
